Practicum

Objectives

The following is a list of practicum objectives set out before the beginning of my on-site hours, which started on August 16th, 2019.

  1. To become familiar with the facilities, services, relevant committees, and management of the school library media center at Stow-Munroe Falls High School
  2. To become familiar with the services the school library media specialist provides, such as assisting students and other library users with the use of technology, answering reference questions, and helping students use print, non-print, and online library resources
  3. To become familiar with the school library media center’s collection and its cataloging practices and procedures
  4. To observe instruction sessions, including information literacy instruction and library use instruction
  5. To collaborate with the practicum advisor and other school personnel to help plan instruction sessions that are helpful and relevant to student needs
  6. To collaborate with teachers at all steps to successfully complete a project that will benefit the school and provide experience working with other educators
  7. To successfully conduct an in-service training for educators at Stow-Munroe Falls High School and receive constructive feedback which will help me with future in-service programs
  8. To complete the required project to identify assistive technology; evaluating the school’s accessibility to students with special needs by applying the principles of universal design
  9. To learn about professional development opportunities and how to pursue continuing education
  10. Overall, to gain broad experience during my 250 on-site hours and learn practical skills that benefit my future career as a school library media specialist

2. Collaborative Project

 "Practicum students are required to participate in a collaborative project with one or more teachers during the practicum. The student should be actively involved in the development, implementation, and evaluation of this project. Evidence of implementing the collaborative project must be provided in the student’s electronic portfolio. The academic practicum advisor (Dr. Harper) will follow up with the onsite practicum supervisor for feedback." This project, planned and completed alongside the school librarian who is also a 9th grade Career-Based Intervention class teacher, aligns with ODE-ELA and modern AASL standards. The project consisted of a collaborative instruction unit centering on the use of paired-texts. Students were assigned side-by-side graphic adaptations and traditional texts to determine whether the graphic texts assisted their reading comprehension. An informal experiment was then conducted analyzing test data to determine whether there was a noticeable improvement in performance on multiple-choice and extended response reading comprehension questions.

3. In-service: Media Literacy Issues and Instruction

 "Practicum students are required to conduct some type of teacher training, workshop, or in-service session during the practicum. This can be a session held before or after normal school hours, as part of an in-service day, or via an online module. It is requested that participants in the training/workshop/in-service session fill out a student-designed feedback evaluation rubric with an option for anonymous completion. This evaluation will be included as evidence in the e-portfolio." This in-service used the same PowerPoint as the ILI session, but the presentation and survey were modified for practitioners, specifically the Fulbright Scholars visiting SMFHS, hosted through KSU. Media literacy was a required component of their program, and I was asked to adapt and conduct my news literacy session for them by the librarian, who was KSU's liaison within SMFHS. I received 3/5 anonymous responses to my feedback survey, the feedback for which was positive.
