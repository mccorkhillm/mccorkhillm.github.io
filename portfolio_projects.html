Teen Library Services

Project Title Project Annotation Project Download(s)

Book Talks

 For LIS 60626, Materials and Services to Teens, I wrote and presented 3 booktalks on various young adult works. I tried to choose a theme with which I am familiar (mythology and folklore), but in varying formats and with differing protagonists in order to maintain interest and reach the widest number of teens possible. These booktalks, for which I drafted and praciticed scripts, were delivered to several peers as well as the LIS 60626 instructor. I tried to use different techniques and "hooks," again, to reach the broadest audience, but also to practice with widest variety of skills I could.
  • Booktalk Preparation Sheets (with scripts) Word Document

Pride Month Program

 Another project completed for LIS 60626, this program could be implemented in a public library or a school library (with administrative approval) and seeks to address the needs of an underserved population. This program is hypothetically situated in a public library system that is local to me, and also relies on partnerships with community organizations. Thus, it is a good illustration of the kinds of collaborations I might participate in as a school librarian. The planning process also involved marketing materials, budgeting and funding.

Book Reviews

 As I did in LIS 60629, I completed a series of young adult book reviews for LIS 60626. This broaded my reading diet and helped me to bring current my knowledge of YA literature. Information Literacy Project

Project Title Project Annotation Project Download(s)

Intro to News Literacy: Misinformation, Disinformation, and Evaluating a News Story

 This is an instruction session created as the final project for LIS 60618, Information Literacy Instruction. This project addresses the AASL's "Curate" frame and "Create" competency, which states that students must be able to assess the validity and accuracy of information, an increasingly important topic in today's information landscape and one which is integral to information literacy teaching. It involved a four-step planning process for a 20-minute lesson which was then taught to classmates virtually. This project represents my first experience teaching and receiving feedback, which will be invaluable in my future as a teacher librarian.

Advocacy Podcast

 This podcast was created for LIS 60607 and focuses on increasing stakeholder buy-in in libraries. The focus of the podcast is to highlight how librarians improve academic achievement and what kinds of resources and expertise they provide to do so. Ultimately, it is extremely important to advocate for the profession to key stakeholders such as school administration. Regarding technological use, it required the use of various recording technologies, such as Audacity, and equipment, such as a condenser microphone.

Picturebook App Evaluation

 This project was completed for LIS 60675, Youth Literature in the Digital Realm. I was tasked with using and evaluating picture book apps intended for use by or with children. I evaluated aspects such as ease of use, educational value, and COPPA compliance. Because student-aged children are becoming increasingly exposed to technology, it is vital for information professionals to be able to evaluate and recommend developmentally appropriate and worthwhile apps and digital resources and activities. This exercise helped me to become more familiar with the challenges and important aspects of evaluating digital media for children.

Sample School Library Cataloging Wiki

 For LIS 60624, Cataloging in School Library, I created a wiki using Google Sites to simulate a central source for cataloging and processing specifications for a hypothetical institution. As an essential management skill, I found this project to be challenging in striking a balance between adequate description and too much information.
